Why will people come to your site?

People will come to my site to learn more about building computers and the parts used to build them. They will be coming to use my services and website to plan out a computer build or receive help with ones they have already had or have built. I want to be able to have people come to websites to answer questions and help them with the skills understanding I have in building and working on computers.

What questions will your visitors expect your site to answer for them?

I expect my site to answer questions about computer parts, computer building. I expect my site to be able to answer the questions of part prices, build prices, and repair prices. I also expect my website to be able to get visitors in touch with myself or lead them in the right direction to answer questions they have that might not be readily available on my site.

What types of activities will visitors want to complete on your site?

They will want to complete computer builds, finding parts and prices that fit their budget and needs. They will be able complete a Q&A or maybe a chat area to ask questions and reach out for help and repairs.

What information is necessary to complete any actions your site offers?

There could be a variety of pieces of information, their budget, any questions they have personally, repairs or ideas needed.

Why will your visitors come to your site instead of another to get this information or complete the actions?

I want people to come to my site because of the ease of navigation, the human element of being able to interact with myself, and have me be able to do the build for them, but not at the same pricier cost of other established companies or custom build shops.
